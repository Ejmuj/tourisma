A Fine tourist guide

Practical information about 8 popular places to visit in London.

1. Buckingham Palace

There is a timed admission system operating in 15 minute time slots. This is the time you select when purchasing your ticket.

A trip to The Buckingham Palace lasts from 2 to 2 and a half hours.

The Palace is busy during the summer and we recommend you pre book your tickets to avoid disappointment.

You are advised to wear comfortable shoes, as the visitor route in summer includes a fairly long walk through the garden to the exit, along a gravel path. The path is approximately 500 meters (1600 feet) in length.

There is no formal dress code required to enter the Palace.

Toilets and baby-care facilities are located in the Garden, this is at the end of the route through the Palace. There are no toilet facilities at the start of the visitor route.

2. London Tower

The Tower is busy during the summer and we recommend you pre book your tickets to avoid disappointment.

If you already have your ticket, arrive at the welcome center.

There is a lot to see at the tower of london so please allow enough time to make the most of your visit. On avarge lasts between 2 to 3 hours.

You are advised to wear comfortable shoes, as you will be walking quite a lot.

The building is 1152 meters squared (3779 feet squared) and 27 meters tall (88 feet).

There is no formal dress code required to enter the tower.

Toilets are located at Brick tower, and behind the jewel house.

3. London Eye

Tickets are cheaper when buying online.

London eye is busy during the summer and we recommend you pre book your tickets to avoid disappointment.

The ride lasts approximately 30 minutes.

The London Eye is a farris wheel that is 135 meters high (442 feet).

There is no formal dress code required to enter the eye of london.

Toilets are located at the ticket office at the London Eye by the VIP desk.

4. Tower Bridge

The Tower Bridge is 240 meters (800 feet) in length.

You can find the toilets at the south tower.

The ride will last about 5 minutes.

5. The British Museum

It is advised to prebook Tickets to the British Museum to avoid disappointment.

21,600 meters squared (990,000 sq. ft) so we advise you to wear som comfortable boot because it will be a lot of walking.

The tour last approximately 90 minutes.

There is no formal dress code to be seen.

If you already have a ticket arrive at the main entrance.

Toilets are available at Great Court, the Ford Centre for Young Visitors, the Clore Education Centre and to the north of Room 66.

6. St.Paul's Cathedral

It is advised to prebook tickets to St Paul's Cathedral to avoid disappointment.

Its 111 meters (365 feet) high and 158 meters long (518 feet).

A tour lasts about 90 minutes.

7. Big Ben

It is advised to Pre Book your ticket to avoid disappointment.

The Big Ben is 96 meters tall (316 feet).

You will have to go about 334 steps.

It lasts approximately 90 minutes.

There are no toilets at the big ben.

There is no formal dress code required to enter the Tower of London.

Visitors should arrive at the Cromwell Green entrance.

8. Trafalgar Square

Trafalgar Square is almost always open.

The center is approximately 52 meters (169 feet).

There are a couple of high quality restaurants in the area.

You will find toilets at the restaurants.

We also recomend that you see the Nelson's Collumn at the Trafalgar Square.
